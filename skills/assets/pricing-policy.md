# NRI Pricing Policy

## Purpose
Internal pricing policy for NRI tax matters. Ensures consistent pricing, margin protection, and alignment between fees and actual file complexity. Use alongside the Intake Questionnaire, Document Checklist, and Pricing & Scope Classifier module.

## Pricing Philosophy
Price for **work required**, not only for the service label the client requests. Most NRI files labeled "just filing" involve more complexity than the client realizes. Pricing must reflect: complexity of facts, number of income streams, residency sensitivity, capital transaction intensity, reconciliation effort, cross-border advisory needs, documentation gaps, and urgency.

## Service Tiers

### Tier 1 — Basic NRI Filing
- Clear profile, one or two limited income streams, low risk
- Includes: basic data review, return preparation, filing, computation summary
- Excludes: advisory memo, treaty/FTC review, complex capital gains, notice support

### Tier 2 — Advisory Filing
- Residency review needed, multiple income types, moderate reconciliation
- Includes: filing, advisory call or short note, moderate review, issue spotting
- Excludes unless separately priced: detailed notice response, complex memo, multi-year review

### Tier 3 — Premium Compliance / Complex Filing
- Property sale, unlisted shares, ESOP, material reconciliation risk
- Includes: detailed review, filing, structured advisory summary, senior/partner review
- Excludes unless specifically included: full notice representation, litigation, multi-year cleanup

### Tier 4 — Retainer / Ongoing NRI Tax Desk
- Annual planning, HNI/family office, recurring advisory
- Includes: periodic advisory access, annual filing, transaction pre-review, planning engagement

## Pricing Bands
- **Band A:** Simple filing lane
- **Band B:** Filing plus moderate advisory
- **Band C:** Complex filing plus specialist review
- **Band D:** Premium / retainer / specialist lane

Exact fee ranges should be mapped by the firm based on geography, target segment, brand positioning, and reviewer seniority.

## Pricing Drivers

**Technical:** Number of income categories, residency ambiguity, property/capital gains, startup equity, business receipts, FTC/DTAA sensitivity.

**Review Effort:** Document volume, follow-ups, reconciliation effort, advisory memo, specialist review.

**Risk:** Material mismatch risk, prior notice/scrutiny, weak documentation, unsupported assertions.

**Commercial:** Urgency, repeat vs new client, one-time vs recurring, bundled services.

## Upward Adjustments
Consider upward adjustment where:
- Urgent deadline or compressed turnaround
- Incomplete or poorly organized records
- High-value property or share transaction
- Multiple follow-up rounds expected
- Prior litigation or notice history
- Cross-border documents require review
- Specialist memo or partner review needed

## Downward Adjustments
Consider limited downward adjustment only where:
- Client is repeat and well-documented
- File is genuinely low-complexity
- Documents are complete and organized
- Filing is bundled with recurring or family work

Do not reduce price merely because the client says the case is simple.

## Add-On Services (Price Separately Unless Explicitly Bundled)
- Advisory memo
- Consultation call beyond standard scope
- Notice reply support
- Revised filing due to late-disclosed transactions
- Multi-year clean-up
- Treaty / FTC deeper review
- Capital gains specialist analysis
- Document deficiency follow-up beyond reasonable limit

## Scope Protection Rules
Every quote or engagement must clearly state:
- What is included
- What is excluded
- What assumptions pricing is based on
- That scope may change if additional facts or documents emerge
- That specialist or notice support is separate unless included

## Quoting Workflow
1. **Intake Review:** Review questionnaire and classify complexity.
2. **Document Status Check:** Complete, partial, or weak.
3. **Service Tier Assignment:** Tier 1 / 2 / 3 / 4.
4. **Pricing Band Assignment:** Band A / B / C / D.
5. **Scope Note:** Inclusions, exclusions, assumptions, timeline.
6. **Escalation if Needed:** Do not quote complex cases at junior level without review.

## Example Pricing Logic
- **NRO interest only, clean documents:** Tier 1, Band A
- **Rent + interest + moderate residency review:** Tier 2, Band B
- **Property sale + TDS mapping + incomplete papers:** Tier 3, Band C
- **Foreign tax credit + residency sensitivity + stock compensation:** Tier 3 or 4, Band C or D

## Internal Guardrails
- Do not quote complex property, startup equity, or cross-border files as basic filing.
- Do not include notice support by default unless intentional.
- Do not treat missing papers as "manageable later" where they affect defensibility.
- Do not lock low pricing before intake and document status are reviewed.
- Do not allow urgency to erase review discipline.

## External Positioning
Position the offering as:
- NRI Tax Filing + Advisory
- End-to-End NRI Tax Compliance
- Premium NRI Tax Review and Filing Desk

Reinforce that the service is advisory-led, defensible, and workflow-driven — not mass-market return preparation.
